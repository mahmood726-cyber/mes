Multiverse Evidence Synthesis: Robustness Classification of 403 Cochrane Meta-Analyses Across 648 Analytical Specifications

Do meta-analysis conclusions change when all defensible analytical specifications are explored systematically rather than a single model reported? The Multiverse Evidence Synthesis framework was validated on 403 Cochrane systematic reviews from the Pairwise70 dataset, each containing three or more eligible studies. The three-phase ASSESS-EXPLORE-MAP pipeline executes 648 or more specifications per review across six dimensions: heterogeneity estimator, confidence interval method, bias correction, quality filter, design filter, and leave-one-out sensitivity. The mean concordance score was 0.763 (SD 0.183, IQR 0.647 to 1.000), with 161 reviews (40.0%) classified as Fragile or Unstable across the robustness tiers. Bias correction was the dominant variance source in 399 of 403 reviews (99.0%), with a mean dominant eta-squared of 0.929 explaining nearly all specification variance. MES replaces single-model point estimates with robustness-classified evidence landscapes, revealing how much of the analytical space supports each conclusion. The framework is limited to pairwise comparisons and cannot currently accommodate network meta-analysis or diagnostic test accuracy specifications.
